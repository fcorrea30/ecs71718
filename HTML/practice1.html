Rosa Parks

"The mother of the freedom movement"


Rosa Parks played a very crucial part in the civil rights movement during the 1950s-1960s.
She famously refused to give up her seat to a white man and did not move to the back of the bus.
Her actions lead to a boycott of the public transport of Mongomery, Alabama for 381 days.

Achievments

    1.Rosa Louise McCauley was born in Tuskegee, Alabama, on February 4, 1913.
    2.Rosa refused to give up her seat to a white man on the bus and was arrested, sparking the the montgomery bus boycott.
    3.Rosa co-founded the Rosa and Raymond Parks Institute for Self-Development in 1987.
    4.She made good friends with Martin Luther King Jr. and aided in the civil rights movement along with him.
    5.Rosa wrote a autobiography by the name of “Rosa Parks: My Story.”
